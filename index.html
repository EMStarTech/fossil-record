How It Works
Everything you need to know to get started
🚀 The 5-Step Workflow
1
Set up your User Profile
Go to User Profile in the sidebar. Fill in your background, communication style, and working philosophy. This is your Relational Pre-Cache — it travels with every prompt you generate, briefing the receiving AI on who you are before the session begins. Do this once. Update it anytime.
2
Paste your AI conversation
Go to AI Extractor in the sidebar. Copy your conversation from Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini — any AI platform — and paste it into the transcript box.
3
Click AI Extract
Claude reads your transcript and extracts seven structural sections — Session Summary, Primary Failure Variable, Load-Bearing Joints, Decisions, Open Questions, Context, and Node Drift Detector markers.
4
Generate your Re-Entry Prompt
Click Generate Prompt. Fossil Record creates a compact structured summary including your Node Drift Detector and User Profile — everything the receiving AI needs.
5
Copy and paste into any AI
Click COPY, open a new session in Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini — whatever you use — and paste your prompt. The AI instantly recovers full context and you pick up exactly where you left off.
📖 What Do The Terms Mean?
🔩
Load-Bearing Joints
The structural foundations of your conversation — the key assumptions, dependencies, or constraints that everything else is built on. If a Joint is wrong, the whole plan can collapse. Example: "The entire project assumes the budget won't change before Q3."
📌
Decisions & Commitments
Things that were explicitly agreed, confirmed, or finalized. These are done — no longer up for debate. They prevent the AI from relitigating resolved issues. Example: "Confirmed: LinkedIn is the primary launch channel."
❓
Open Questions
Things that were raised but not resolved — deferred decisions, unresolved debates, items flagged for follow-up. These are the first things your next session should address. Example: "Unresolved: Should the newsletter launch now or wait for 500 customers?"
📋
Context
Background information that shaped the conversation — facts, constraints, or situational details the AI needs to give relevant responses next session. Example: "The client has a hard deadline of March 15th."
🔍
Node Drift Detector
A cognitive fingerprint for the receiving AI — behavioral markers extracted from the session that help detect when the AI is drifting from your established patterns, logic, or constraints. Includes hard stop tripwires and a human-side checklist for you to run periodically. Example: "Flag if AI advances topics without explicit release from user."
🧬
User Profile (Relational Pre-Caching)
Your persistent professional identity — set once in User Profile, travels with every prompt you generate. Tells the receiving AI not just what the project needs but who is driving it. Includes your background, communication style, non-negotiables, philosophy, AI relationship preferences, and anti-gravity statement.
🗄 What Is The Vault?
The Vault is your permanent library of Fossil Records. Every record you save is stored here and accessible anytime — even after you close the browser and come back days later. After generating a re-entry prompt, give your record a name, choose a project, and click Save to Vault.
💡
Pro Tip — The Token Savings
Instead of copy-pasting your entire previous conversation into a new session, use your Fossil Record re-entry prompt. In a real test, an 11,377 word conversation was reduced to a ~500 token prompt — a 97% token reduction — without losing a single important idea.
